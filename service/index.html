Service Notes on DKK 416K

Having bought the B in November 2025, and with the MoT certificate due to expire in June of 2026, I decided in May to give the car it's first service under my ownership. A list of jobs can be found by clicking on the image to the right.

I started by changing the engine coolant. Unfortunately there is no drain tap on the radiator, so you have to remove the bottom hose. I had to remove the front air filter assembly so as to get access and then leave the hose to 'relax' for a while before I could get it off the radiator. And even then it was very hard work!

My second problem was that I couldn't undo the drain bolt on the engine block. The bolt is just behind the distributor. My 1/2" socket extension was too long, the steering column U/J getting in the way, and my 1/4" sockets were too short, the distributor getting in the way. I found an old box spanner that fitted the bolt, another that fitted the first and a tommy bar, but I still couldn't shift it. I later discovered that even Doug at Simms garage couldn't undo it! So I ran the engine for a short while to pump out what I could and then topped up the radiator with an ethylene glycol mix.

I jacked up the front of the car using the trolley jack under the cross member (with the rear two lugs behind the cross member), but couldn't see where best to place the jack stands. Later I discovered that, if you want the suspension lowered, the best place is on the frame rails, just behind where they curve upwards. But I could see that reaching the sump drain plug would be tricky. Then I jacked up the rear and put stands under the front of the springs. I found that I couldn't reach all the bolts on the differential cover (I wanted to replace the gasket) and I couldn't undo the exhaust pipe clamp bolts to replace it.

So doing the service jobs would be more difficult than I thought. Consequently I decided to ask Doug at Simms to do them.

My original list of jobs was:

  • Replace hydraulic hose between clutch master and slave cylinders.
  • Drain engine oil, replace oil filter, fit new drain plug washer, fill with 4.26 litres Miller 20W50 oil.
  • Drain gearbox and overdrive oil and refill with 3.36 litres of Miller 20W50 oil.
  • Drain differential and remove rear plate. Refit plate with double gaskets using Wellseal jointing compound on all surfaces. Refill with 0.85 litres of EP90.
  • Free coolant drain plug on engine block (rear of distributor).
  • Grease front suspension - 3 points each side.
  • Grease prop shaft U/Js and spline.
  • Grease handbrake cable.
  • Check rear brake shoes and adjust as necessary.
  • Replace exhaust pipe rear U-clamp.
  • Check windscreen washer (driver side not working).
  • Check brake lights (seem dim).
  • Do MoT test.

In the process of doing these jobs, Doug found that:

  • The two rear brake cylinders were leaking.
  • The nearside rear damper to spring link had parted from its rubber insert and so was hanging free.
  • The brake switch was faulty.
  • The clutch slave cylinder could not be bled.
  • The clutch master cylinder was leaking.

Consequently the following jobs were added to the list:

  • Replace the two rear brake slave cylinders.
  • Replace the nearside rear damper to spring link.
  • Replace the brake switch.
  • Replace the clutch master and slave cylinders.

All parts and materials, such as oils and filter, were provided by me. Doug also replaced all the brake and clutch fluid and commented that the paint around the brake master cylinder (which I had cleaned up) had come off due to the fluid leak.
